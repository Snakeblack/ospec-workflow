# ADR-003: Executable reducers define selective gate behavior

- Status: proposed
- Change: selective-4r-generalist-review
- Date: 2026-07-16

## Context
Verification found that prose contracts and classifier tests did not prove dispatch, fail-closed state, legacy compatibility, or bounded termination.

## Decision
Use pure CommonJS reducers at the gate boundary. `review-gate-state.js` plans selective dispatch and audit actions; `review-lineage.js` owns immutable review authority, legal transitions, correction budgets, and targeted validation. Neither performs I/O or launches agents, and neither authorizes a full reviewer after findings freeze.

## Alternatives
- Markdown-only orchestration: not executable and already left MUST scenarios unproved.
- Test-only fake gate: can drift from production behavior.
- Add state I/O to the classifier: mixes evidence selection with persistence policy.
- Keep owner rereview: permits new discovery to reopen the same review indefinitely.

## Consequences
Gate behavior and termination become fixture-testable and portable across five targets; the orchestrator remains the I/O adapter. Both reducers are generated runtime contracts and must pass parity and negative-drift tests. Reversal requires restoring prose-owned orchestration and losing executable termination proof.
